TAG Project Updates

This morning, arriving at TAG, I met with Ben, who I had previously worked with on assets for real estate clients including Hali, Adamson, M3, and Hall & Hart. Specifically, creating After Effects renders for their clients' properties.

There had been news that one of M3's properties had sold, and therefore they needed to change the ad copy on the posts to advertise the successful sale. I created two versions of the motion graphic in both square and vertical format.

Over the last month, as I knew I would be working within After Effects, I have been taking a course by Australian YouTuber Ben Marriott. Throughout this series of tutorials, I learned about folder hierarchies and the importance of maintaining different precompositions that can be pieced together in final render comp files for the different ratios a client may need. This ensures that if there are future points of feedback, there is only one set of changes that needs to be made.

I discovered today, while accessing files I had worked on previously, there was a lack of organisation throughout the workspace, and the two ratios had completely different main sequences and subsequences for each aspect ratio. However, I managed to find a workaround where I manually separated the different pre-comp layers and successfully merged the file so that I didn't have to double my workload.

Below I have uploaded my animation as a animated GIF however the deliverable was a high resolution video in different aspect ratios.

Moose Toys Project Process

Yesterday, on my day off, I headed to the city to meet with a friend for lunch. Afterward, I went to Big W to check out if there were any relevant toys for me to experiment with developing into an Augmented Reality filter.

I found the Moose Toy brand there with their different licensed brands: Mr. Beast and Bluey. I bought one of the Bluey sets as I thought it would be good to experiment with and create an example/mockup to take to the meeting in a few weeks.

I presented this work in progress, explained the lengthy process necessary for 3D modeling, retopology, texturing, etc., and discussed my reasoning behind choosing these toys instead of a different off-brand toy or other product. Namely because using official products avoids questions about whether the work was created for a different client that had signed on.

I also made a very short sketch to illustrate what I plan on animating.
