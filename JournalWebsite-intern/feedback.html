Initial Analysis

  • The Rokoko system is not as simple to adapt as advertised. I know some people that have used these and the animations require large amounts of labour time to conduct manual cleanup.
  • Real-time AI mocap remains jittery and unrefined, mostly suited for VR chat rooms or experimental art unless using advanced multi-camera setups.

Post Production Enhancement

The setting up of a usable green screen studio is a bit more difficult and requires someone to be more considerate of the lighting in the space that has experience with lighting a green screen space or studio.

Issues include shadows, uneven lit backdrop and colour spill.

Haptic Gloves are not necessary and are not standardised or implementable in any feasible or worthwhile way.

Implementable Augmented Reality Systems in a Post-TikTok World

The past week has seen the closure of Meta's Spark AR Platform for developing augmented reality experiences and the company has cut all third parties from releasing new content of this kind.

The past week has also seen the banning of TikTok across the US. Notably tiktok is also banned in Pakistan, Afghanistan and India. Looking forward the future of this platform is unsteady and the supported services that will survive the transition are not clear. Therefore I propose an implementation of WebXR, MindAR and 3.js. All are open source libraries for javascript that can be implemented across the web and within React applications for future app development. This system works across most platforms including Android, IOS and PC.
