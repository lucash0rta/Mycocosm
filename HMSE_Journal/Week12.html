Notes on Futurology and Media Theory

Wednesday Notes: Photography and Futurology

Some notes and thoughts after reading on wednesday on futurology and the way vilem flusser talks about photography.

- But it is also a project: it is how future photographs might be. Therefore, it is an aspect of the future. The sum of all the possible mirror reflections is the universe of all possible photographs, and what the photographer sees while looking for a point of view is a specific section of the future. This is futurology, not prophecy, because it projects the present into the future and thus preempts the future. Prophecy is the opposite motion: it projects the future into the present and thus preempts the present. In other words, the decision of the photographer to press the button is a decision in favor of a future aspect he likes.

- Is this not the larger game: positioning ourselves in MMOs, playing roles, collecting achievements to fulfill our dream? The collective decision-making leads through this predictable future, like the apparatus-operator complex with the camera. We see aesthetics, styles, and character customizability. We exist in a form of futurology, schismed across time. Even when we exist in the now, that moment is one we prescribe to ourselves, necessitating some idea of rest that plays into the wider game.

Thursday Notes: Extended Thoughts on Communicology

Then on thursday after finishing the book I decided to write out some rambling ideas and notes connecting some readings throughout the semester into a longer more cohesive thought.

Today I finished the final chapter of Communicology by Vilem Flusser, I had been wanting to read this book for some time but it is now that I realise how well it really explains our current relationship with history, with time, space and media. One of the most poignant parts of the final chapter was how history is no longer linear, that we are at the center of space-time and filter history through a view of importance.

"I do not become; I stand always still; it is the world that comes toward me; it becomes me." - Page 192, Communicology

This linear history is, in fact, a fake, mediated history that is being fed back to us through methods of communication, and we draw from the archive as we wish.

"It consists of cutting history and gluing it together again in a way that produces a trompe l'imagination (a movie) in the receivers. It consists of transforming history into a movie story. But nothing is achieved by calling this bad faith on the part of the operators. By transforming true story into trompe-histoire, this sort of recodification shows that true history is just as fictitious as is a movie story and that to commit oneself to history is pure folly." - Page 173 - Communicology

These images flow into one another but now with many layers of feedback loops and market incentives, with AI bots reacting, watching, and playing into recommendation algorithms to enhance performance and reaction. It stands outside of the sphere that Flusser predicted; we are all distorted within the market. Will the mainstream media scape continue down this hole? What will become of history? It reminds me of how Jean Baudrillard speaks about the Vietnam War and the Coppola film Apocalypse Now.

"One revisits everything through cinema and one begins again: the Molochian joy of filming, the sacrificial joy of so many millions spent, of such a holocaust of means, of so many misadventures, and the remarkable paranoia that from the beginning conceived of this film as a historical, global event, in which, in the mind of the creator, the war in Vietnam would have been nothing other than what it is, would not fundamentally have existed - and it is necessary for us to believe in this: the war in Vietnam 'in itself' perhaps in fact never happened, it is a dream, a baroque dream of napalm and of the tropics, a psychotropic dream that had the goal neither of a victory nor of a policy at stake, but, rather, the sacrificial, excessive deployment of a power already filming itself as it unfolded, perhaps waiting for nothing but consecration by a superfilm, which completes the mass-spectacle effect of this war." - Jean Beaudrillard, Apocalypse Now

This double fake, this new understanding of the history and that each person can read the signs and symbols drawn from history through their individual interpretations

"If painting The Scream today, the appropriate backdrop would not be the blood-red sky of Krakatoa's eruption, but a firmament criss-crossed with contrails: the same contrails that litter the websites of chemtrail conspiracy theorists, even, if not especially, those who deny the realities of man-made climate change. We are all looking at the same sky and seeing radically different things." - The New Dark Age, James Bridle

This thinking that we all see the same things, maybe slightly different however that everybody from their individualistic or community driven ideologies identify with and tear apart technical images in their own way sand decipher their meanings through their own lenses is exactly how each of us see the world.

However their may be a new paranoid feeling that pervades the contemporary man if their is an understanding of the technical image and the subversive tactics used by state, private and individuals using the tools of control as they are of free access now.

We can all become grifters in this new world - as mentioned in the new dark age further -

"In 2012, two brothers from Veles set up a website called HealthyFoodHouse.com. They stuffed it with weight-loss tips and recommendations for alternative remedies, culled from wherever they could find them on the internet, and over the years it drew more and more visitors. Their Facebook page has 2 million subscribers, and 10 million come to the site every month, drawn, via Google, to articles with titles like 'How To Get Rid of The Folds On Your Back And Sides in 21 Days' and '5 Soothing Essential Oils To Rub On Your Sciatic Nerve For Instant Pain Relief'. This also links through to the ideas of e-deologies and the subversive tactics that radical teenagers use to leverage the social media algrorithims to expose wider audiences to their content. As mentioned in "The Slow Red Pill" - Joshua Citarella -

"by all outward appearances it would look like a regular conservative Instagram page. Not just are these people using these techniques of the platform to leverage to create an outreach they are purporting their own experience of history and are examples exactly of how fluser describes our new relationship with history and how time is situated with our interest now.

"It is the closer the more I become interested in it (interest = to be involved). The only scale of measurement such a space admits is a scale that measures my interest in the objective world. Such a scale may become as exact as are the scales of classical physics. If one accepts that the mathesis of the objective world is my interest in it, my interest becomes just as quantifiable as are centimeters and seconds" - Communicology, Page 196

This measurement of interest has been the aim of the platforms of the 21st century to quantify, to calculate, to predict and from that form recommendations which aid advertisers in finding their targets, whether via their desires or repulsions to engage with the advertised content. In turn people create content to supply the demands and quantified crossover desires, unexpected or seemingly unexplainable interests, rule 42 and everyone throwing shit at the wall until it sticks then we strike gold in the attention economy, the bots notice it, industries are created, more people are targetted in their own complex venn diagrams of consumer profiles and we see the enshittified internet emerge. Take books: The average Barnes & Noble carries 130,000 titles. Yet more than half of Amazon's book sales come from outside its top 130,000 titles." - Wired, Chris Anderson, October 1st 2004.

We've seen this all play out and now we see history itself enshittified, Ai generated products or niche seemingly weird media created by real humans that seemingly tapped into both the market conditions of the net and the half dazed brains of the consumer binging on mindless gloop.

Communicology Quotes & Notes:

On specialists and knowledge: "The specialists cannot teach us because they themselves do not know what they are doing."

On technical images and mass communication: "Operators of technical images on the elite level of communication have technical imagination, while those on the mass level do not... It is intolerable to be programmed in a state of lack of awareness by operators who themselves lack awareness of what they are doing."

On historical awareness of media: "People were more aware of the meaning of films at the time of the Lumière brothers than they are at present."

On photographer-apparatus relationship: "He uses his apparatus for his decisions, but he decides regarding his apparatus. He does not move with the apparatus or against it; he does not motivate the apparatus, nor is he motivated by it."

Note: The relationship with apparatus and how mediation and understanding of the world through homogenous technology. Phones, word processors, platforms etc. Flattens us as more of our life is filtered through this relationship.

On technical imagination: "Technical imagination is not value-free... but it stands on a level that is different from historical, political, and ethical evaluation: it aims at good photographs of the world."

On sharing photographs: "Photographs are points of view meant to be shared with others, and the more they are thus shared, the truer they are and the better they are."

On TV and conditioning: "The images and sounds that emanate from the TV function like drugs: they are habit forming. The more they supply the receiver with sensations, the more they condition him or her for further consumption."

On human existence and codes: "Human life is meaningless, in a sense applicable only to humans and to no other animal; humans exist, which implies that they know they are going to die. This knowledge of death is knowledge of the ultimate meaninglessness of living. The purpose of codes is to make life meaningful in spite of such knowledge."
